Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (2 October 1869 – 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Early Life & Education

  1. Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, known as Mahatma Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar to a Hindu family.
  2. Gandhi's father was Karamchand Uttamchand Gandhi, who served as the dewan (chief minister) of Porbandar state.
  3. Gandhi started his education in Rajkot and later joined the High School in Rajkot.
  4. He married Kasturbai Gokuldas Kapadia in an arranged marriage at the age of 13.
“Be the change you are trying to create.”

Struggle For Indian Independence

  1. Gandhi led the Indian National Congress and campaigned for easing poverty, expanding women's rights, and achieving self-rule.
  2. He challenged British rule with movements like the Dandi Salt March and calling for the British to quit India.
"My religion is based on truth and non-violence. Truth is my God. Non-violence is the means of realising Him."

Principles & Beliefs

  1. Gandhi's life mission was to discover and pursue truth (Satya).
  2. Satyagraha first became a political movement in 1920, transforming Gandhi into a prominent figure in Indian consciousness.Satyagraha is about "soul force" or "silent force," refusing to use brute force.
  3. Nonviolence is a central ethical value in Indian thought, which Gandhi adapted for political use.
  4. Gandhi emphasized that "God is Truth," later modifying it to "Truth is God," equating truth with the universal Being (Brahman, Atman).
"Happiness is when what you think, what you say, and what you do are in harmony.”

Death

  1. At 5:17 p.m. on 30 January 1948, Gandhi was with his grandnieces in the garden of Birla House (now Gandhi Smriti), on his way to address a prayer meeting, when Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist, fired three bullets into Gandhi's chest from a pistol at close range.
  2. The Birla House site where Gandhi was assassinated is now a memorial called Gandhi Smriti.
  3. The place near Yamuna River where he was cremated is the Rāj Ghāt memorial in New Delhi. A black marble platform, it bears the epigraph "Hē Rāma" (Devanagari: हे ! राम or, Hey Raam). These are said to be Gandhi's last words after he was shot

Awards & recognition

  1. Time magazine named Gandhi the Man of the Year in 1930.
  2. In 2011, Gandhi topped TIME's list of top 25 political icons of all time.
  3. Gandhi was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize five times, and publicly, the committee regretted the omission of awarding him the prize.
  4. Gandhi is widely described as the Father of the Nation in India, and the origin of this title dates back to a radio address in 1944.
  5. Gandhi was a central figure in the 2006 comedy film Lage Raho Munna Bhai.
  6. Gandhi Jayanti on 2nd October is a national holiday in India.
